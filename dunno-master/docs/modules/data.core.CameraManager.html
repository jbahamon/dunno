Basic camera functions

Basic camera functions
CameraManager:setPosition (position)
Centers the camera on a given position.

Parameters:

  • position The position to center the camera on, as a vector.
CameraManager:draw (players)
Draws everything the camera sees.
The camera will draw the stage ands its elements first, and the players on top of them.

Parameters:

  • players The players to draw on the stage, as an array.
CameraManager:setWorld (l, t, w, h)
Sets the bounds of the camera.
The camera will try to not draw anything beyond its bounds, adjusting its position adequately.

Parameters:

  • l The horizontal coordinate of the top-left corner of the camera's bounds.
  • t The vertical coordinate of the top-left corner of the camera's bounds.
  • w The width of the camera's bounds.
  • h The height of the camera's bounds.
CameraManager:start ()
Starts the camera manager, centering the camera in its starting position.

Sets the point to where the camera should point to (i.e. the camera's focus). The focus is set depending on the current camera mode. Currently, the camera manager tries to center itself around the first player in this array.

Screen effects (fading, etcetera).
CameraManager:fadeToColor (rampTime, color, numSteps)
Fades the screen to a solid tint. having discrete intermediate values. If omitted, the transition is done as smoothly as possible.

Parameters:

  • rampTime The time (in seconds) for the screen to get to the tint's full intensity.
  • color The color to apply to all elements, as an array (not a dictionary) of four values, corresponding to red, green, blue, and alpha, in that order.
  • numSteps (Optional) The number of steps for the ramp. Use this to give the fading a "retro" look,
